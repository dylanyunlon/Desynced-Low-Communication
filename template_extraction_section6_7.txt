--- Section 6 Related Work, Para 1 (同步训练与通信削减) ---
In synchronous CCI training, workers exchange full CCII or J CCV iteration, incurring N costs linear in model size using CCIII. When hardware is CCIV or widely distributed, N significantly slows CCXII training time as workers need to wait for R to finish. CCVI and CCVII reduce N by performing K local optimization steps before averaging J, decreasing N rounds by a factor of K. CCVIII extensions to adaptive optimizers either keep A states local or CCIX them after each sync, both lacking robust CCXI guarantees.

--- Section 7 Conclusion, Para 1 ---
A reconciles N efficiency with rigorous CCXI guarantees in distributed adaptive optimization. By extending theory to the independent R of B and CCXX A states, we empirically demonstrate convergence alongside 170× and 2× N reductions over DDP and prior CCXXI methods at CCXXII-scale LLM training, even in environments prone to CCXXIII. Our findings yield clear guidelines: i) frequently synchronize J, and ii) synchronize A states less often, proportional to their CCXXIV. These insights open avenues for future research, including CCXXV, CCXXVI, CCXXVII, as well as emerging applications, such as CCXXVIII and CCXXIX. As training workloads scale, we envision A becoming the standard for efficient, resilient foundation-model training in data centers and distributed environments.
